## HOW TO SET UP IN 12 MINUTES

### Minutes 1-2: Open the File
- Download **The_Paycheck_Reset.xlsx** and open it in Google Sheets or Excel
- Start on the **📋 Start Here** tab — read the 7 steps
- You'll notice there are 6 tabs at the bottom. Go in order.

### Minutes 3-5: Enter Your Income (💰 Monthly Budget Tab)
1. Click on the **💰 Monthly Budget** tab
2. Find the **INCOME section** at the top
3. Enter your monthly take-home pay (after taxes) in the BUDGETED column
   - If you're paid bi-weekly, multiply one paycheck × 2.17 for a monthly average
   - If you have side income, add it too — every dollar counts
4. The ACTUAL column is where you fill in real numbers as the month progresses

**Pro tip:** Start with BUDGETED amounts (what you plan to spend). Update ACTUAL at the end of each week.

### Minutes 6-9: Fill In Your Fixed Expenses
Fixed expenses don't change month to month:
- **Rent / Mortgage** — your biggest number, be honest
- **Utilities** — electric, gas, water. Check last 3 months and average them.
- **Internet** — that's a fixed cost
- **Car payment** — exact number from your loan statement
- **Insurance** — health, car, renters
- **Subscriptions** — don't skip this. Go to the 🔍 Subscription Audit tab first.

### Minutes 10-12: Estimate Variable Expenses
These change month to month. Use your best guess for now:
- **Groceries** — most people underestimate. Try $300-500/month per adult.
- **Dining out** — be honest. Check your bank app history.
- **Gas / transportation**
- **Entertainment**

Don't stress about being exact. You'll refine over the first 2-3 months.

## UNDERSTANDING YOUR RESULTS

### The "Money Left Over" Cell
At the bottom of the budget, you'll see **MONEY LEFT OVER** highlighted in green.

- **Positive number?** Great. Decide NOW where that money goes — savings, debt payoff, or a specific goal. Don't let it disappear into "miscellaneous."
- **Negative number?** That means you're planning to overspend. Time to find cuts.

### The DIFFERENCE Column
This shows ACTUAL minus BUDGETED.
- **Green/positive** = you spent LESS than planned ✓
- **Red/negative** = you spent MORE than planned. Figure out why.

## THE DEBT SNOWBALL (💳 Tab)

### How to Fill It In
1. List every debt you have (credit cards, student loans, car loan, medical bills)
2. Enter the current balance
3. Enter the interest rate (find it on your statement)
4. Enter the minimum monthly payment
5. Enter any EXTRA you can put toward debt

**Watch the magic happen:** The MONTHS TO PAYOFF column calculates automatically. And the **DEBT-FREE DATE** cell shows you exactly when you'll be done — down to the month.

### The Snowball Method (Why It Works)
1. Pay minimums on ALL debts
2. Put every extra dollar toward the SMALLEST balance first
3. When smallest is paid off, roll that payment to the next smallest
4. Each payoff gives you more momentum (and more cash for the next debt)

Studies show the psychological wins of eliminating debts one-by-one are worth more than the mathematical efficiency of paying highest-interest first. You're more likely to stick with it.

## THE BI-WEEKLY SPLITTER (📅 Tab)

**Problem:** Most people are paid every two weeks, but bills are due monthly. This mismatch causes overdrafts.

**Solution:** Enter your per-paycheck take-home amount in the yellow cell. The splitter shows exactly what bills to pay from Paycheck 1 vs. Paycheck 2.

**Action step:** Set up automatic bill payments to come out the day after each paycheck lands. You'll never overdraft on a bill again.

## TROUBLESHOOTING

**"My pie chart isn't showing up"**
The Money Map tab's chart uses example data by default. As you fill in the budget tab, the chart auto-updates. If using Google Sheets, you may need to reconnect the chart data range.

**"The debt payoff date says 'Enter data'"**
Make sure you've filled in the balance, interest rate, minimum payment, AND extra payment columns. All four are needed for the calculation.

**"I don't know my interest rates"**
Log into each credit card or loan account online. The rate is usually on the homepage dashboard. For federal student loans, check studentaid.gov.

**"My actual expenses are always different from my budget"**
That's normal for the first 1-2 months. The budget becomes accurate through repetition. Keep tracking for 60 days and you'll have a realistic picture.
